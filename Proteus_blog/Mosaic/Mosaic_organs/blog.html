How many Organs can a Human live without?

June 9, 2022 | 4 min read

-The inspiring story of Salma Bashir-

While surfing the internet for the sake of this article, I happened to stumble upon a BBC science focus page that reports the following. “You can still have a fairly normal life without one of your lungs, a kidney, your spleen, appendix, gallbladder, adenoids, tonsils, plus some of your lymph nodes, the fibula bones from each leg and six of your ribs.” The article further mentions how we could artificially replace, through medications and technologies, organs accounting for about 45% of one’s total body mass. That being said, Surgically removing these organs one at a time over many months would likely end up fatal as a consequence of their immune-compromised state.

The focus of this article, however, is a girl called Salma Bashir, whose story is heartbreaking and inspirational withal. Oh, and on a side note, this is the story that made me go from “I am doomed.” to “What I’m stressing about isn’t actually a big deal.” on the night before my Chemistry end sem examination.

Four-year-old Salma enjoying her vacation at a public pool in Alexandria, Egypt, had an encounter that could only be exclaimed about as “Oh my god! How could that happen?” She accidentally sat on the kiddie pool’s uncovered suction valve. The suction force was strong enough to glue her still underwater. Yes! This girl had a real-life ‘Final Destination’ moment. When Salma was finally rescued and brought out of the pool, her Mom had to witness something horrendous. What she thought was pieces of her daughter’s swimwear lying on the floor was actually the child's intestines falling out.

Salma was rushed to a hospital in Egypt from where she was sent to Children’s Hospital in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania. After eighteen months and a generous amount of anonymous donations, Salma was able to get her small bowel transplant. However, luck didn't side with her this time either.

Salma had an immune response, and her body rejected the transplant. As if the poor girl had not gone through enough, the infection spread to her large intestine and gallbladder, which had to be removed as well. Salma’s bladder ruptured during the procedure, leaving her incontinent. The surgery also left her with a leaking hole in her stomach that won't heal.

It’s been 13 years since Salma could ingest food through her mouth. She has a Central Venous Catheter (CVC) inserted into her and receives her nutrition from a TPN (total parenteral nutrition) which she has to carry around 20 hours a day. It’s heartbreaking to hear her say, “I don't even remember what it’s like to swallow the food.” Sadly, the TPN does severely damage her liver and possibly her kidney.

Salma currently requires a multi-visceral (five organ) transplant estimated to cost $3 million. Yet, She has not lost heart. She dauntlessly fights her destiny. Despite the traumatising accident, Salma still goes swimming. She’s passionate about makeup and makes frequent appearances on Instagram and Youtube. She’s set up her own go-fund-me page, seeking to raise the huge fortune which could give her back her ‘normal life’.

And I must say, for someone missing most of her gut, Salma indeed has a lot of guts.
