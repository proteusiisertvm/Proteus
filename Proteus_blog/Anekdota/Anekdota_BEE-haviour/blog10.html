Proper Bee-haviour in the Lab

October 18, 2022 | 3 min read

Entomologists and ecologists who are interested in social insects have a popular organism of interest: bees! In labs that rear and study the behaviour of these bees, it never gets boring! Many amusing incidents abound in these labs. Let's have a peek into some extra-cool moments!

Brittany Berdy, a research scientist at the Broad Institute of the MIT and Harvard, did her postdoctoral work on the honey bee microbiome and thus developed a passion for bees and beekeeping. One of her personal favourite hilarious instances was when they had seven hives outside on a roof deck. The bees would follow the researchers back into the building, which obviously, not everyone liked.

“So my colleague Claire and I would go to one end of the deck and wait for all the bees to congregate around us. Then one of us would run as fast as we could across the deck, hoping to leave the bees behind us, and jump inside.”

It must have been an odd sight to the people watching from outside two figures in full bee suits covered with gloves and face coverings running on an open roof deck near the Charles river!

In another exciting bee story, we have “Elementary”, the CBS show starring Jonny Lee Miller as Sherlock Holmes, featuring bees in many episodes. The team managed to keep these beehives well-fed and in observation. Elementary‘s Holmes kept his bees in a unique apiary on the roof of his brownstone. He would spend his idle hours monitoring a society that depends upon order and assigned roles.

These bees were a constant source of amusement and wonder for all those on set, and all such similar shows featuring bees have been the same!
