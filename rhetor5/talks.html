Dr Manu Johns Chowallur
Considering the popular request for a second talk after the thrilling session on forensic biology delivered by Dr Manu, Proteus is organising another exciting talk by Dr Manu. Dr Manu Johns Chowallur earned his MBBS in 2010 and MD in 2015, both from the Govt. Medical College, Thrissur. He went on to study a basic life support course at WIMS, Wayanad, in 2016, and a foundation course in palliative care for doctors under NRHM in 2017. Currently, he is working as assistant professor and assistant police surgeon, Department of Forensic Medicine, Govt. Medical College, Thrissur. He has received several prestigious awards, including the ‘Best Research Paper Award’ in an Indian Academy of Forensic Medicine conference in 2014, KUHS university first rank for MD in forensic medicine in 2015, and first rank in PSC. He has also presented research papers in national and international medico-legal conferences.

Dr. NIBEDITA LENKA
Dr. C.N.Ramchand holds a Ph.D. in biochemistry from MS University Baroda. He also served as an assistant professor and obtained his M. Phil from the same University. He holds a Master’s degree in Chemistry from the University of Kerala. He is also the 3-time winner of the Young Scientist award from Charing Cross and Westminster Medical School, UK for his work on the role of membrane pathology in schizophrenia and the development of EPA as a therapy for schizophrenia. He currently is the CEO and president of Saksin Lifesciences and is also on the board of MagGenome Technologies.

Dr. MRIDULA NAMBIAR
Professor Anil Shaji is an associate professor at the School of Physics in IISER Thiruvananthapuram. He is a member of the American Physical Society and an awardee of the CSIR - India, golden jubilee merit fellowship, 1991. He received his B.Sc. in physics from University College Thiruvananthapuram and his M.Sc. from IIT-Madras. He went on to complete his PhD at the University of Texas, Austin alongside Prof. E. C. G. Sudarshan on open quantum systems and quantum information. After his postdoc at the University of New Mexico, working in the information physics group with Prof. Carlton M. Caves, he joined the School of Physics in June 2009. His research revolves around studying physical phenomena from the perspective of quantum information theory. Presently, he is working on the fundamental and practical limits on measurement precision in quantum metrology. The spin-statistics connection, the quantum Zeno effect and quantum entanglement in continuous-variable systems are some of the topics that he has worked on in the past and wishes to pursue further.

Dr. KULBHUSHANSINGH SURYAVANSHI
Gregory Youdan Jr., MA, MS, is an experienced human movement scientist with a demonstrated history of working in the higher education and non-profit industries. A graduate of the dance program at Hofstra University, he holds masters degrees in motor learning/motor control and applied statistics from Teachers College, Columbia University, where he served as the lab manager for the Neurorehabilitation Research Lab. Skilled in gait, biomechanics, motor learning, motor control, dance science and statistics, Greg uses data science techniques to analyze human movement, especially in developing dance programs for communities with neurodegenerative diseases.

Dr. JYOTHILAKSHMI VADASSERY
Prof. Dr. Vina Vaswani, Director, Centre for Ethics, Yenepoya University, is an eminent bioethicist with a background in forensic medicine, forensic anthropology, bioethics, and toxicology. She completed her Masters (MA) in Bioethics in 2008, as an Erasmus Mundus scholar from three European Universities (Belgium, Netherlands, and Italy). She also has postgraduate diplomas in medical law & ethics and human rights law, both from the National Law School of India University, Bengaluru. She is currently doing her Ph.D. from Dublin City University, Dublin, Ireland.

Dr. VINAY BULUSU
Professor M. K. Mathew heads a laboratory of membrane biophysics at the National Centre for Biological Sciences, Tata Institute of Fundamental Research. In addition, he works on physiology and structural biology. He did his BSc in zoology from the University of Calicut, Kerala, MSc in biotechnology from Bharathiar University, Tamil Nadu, and Ph.D. from National Institute of Technology - Calicut, Kerala. MKM Laboratory focuses mainly on the studies of transmembrane proteins. Structural elucidation of potassium channels, plant anatomy and abiotic stress are his areas of research interest.

Dr. SUJA P DEVIPRIYA
Chinmaya Ghaekar is a conservative biologist who specializes in the study of marine life. She has done her Master’s from Abasaheb Garware College of Arts and Science, Pune, and is currently a Ph.D. scholar at the Wildlife Institute of India, Dehradun. She has been working on dugongs, which are marine herbivores, and has written a paper on the diversity and unique genetic lineage of Indian dugongs. Ms. Ghaekar is also involved in various efforts to conserve the marine life form.

Dr. RAVI DEVANI
Mala Thapa is a researcher in the Agricultural and Ecological Research Unit (AERU) at the Indian Statistical Institute (ISI). Her work involves the application of nanotechnology in agriculture, focusing on the synthesis and characterization of nanoparticles for augmenting plant growth and nutritional qualities. Besides nanotechnology, her areas of expertise include plant physiology, metabolism and biochemistry. She is interested in the mechanism of uptake, translocation and desorption behavior of nanoparticles inside plant cells and the effects of nanoparticles on plant-bacteria symbiosis.

Dr. YASHRAJ CHAVHAN
Dr Vinesh Vijayan secured a bachelor's degree in Chemistry from Mahatma Gandhi University, Kottayam, in 1999. He earned his master’s degree from the Indian Institute of Technology, Madras, in 2002. He went on to complete his PhD and his post-doctoral research from Max Planck Institute for Biophysical Chemistry, Göttingen. He joined IISER Thiruvananthapuram as an Assistant Professor in 2010 and is currently working as an Associate Professor in the School of Chemistry. His group focuses on NMR Structural studies of small molecules and Biomolecules and on structure determination of membrane proteins.
